Hydropower: Harnessing Moving Water

Hydropower, or hydro-electricity, is electricity generated from the flow of water. It is a highly reliable renewable energy source, often providing baseline power to electrical grids.

In conventional hydroelectric power, water is stored in a reservoir behind a dam. When needed, the water is released and flows through a penstock (pipe) to turn a turbine connected to a generator.

Other forms include run-of-river hydro systems and pumped-storage hydropower, which uses electricity to pump water to a higher reservoir for later use.
